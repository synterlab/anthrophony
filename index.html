About ANTHROPHONY
A Playable Archive of Human Cultural Memory
ANTHROPHONY is not a music streaming platform. It is an interactive cultural exploration game where users investigate humanity through sound.
Our Mission

We believe the acoustic traditions of the world are among the most important archives of human civilization — more immediate than text, more emotionally direct than images. ANTHROPHONY exists to preserve, share, and make learnable these 1,000+ acoustic traditions before they disappear.

What Makes Us Different

📖

Progression System

The Science of Sound DNA

Our proprietary Sound DNA system maps acoustic traditions by their measurable characteristics — scale intervals, rhythmic density, vocal technique, instrumentation, and geographic origin. This allows us to draw cultural similarity maps that reveal migration patterns, trade routes, and cultural diffusion invisible to the naked ear.

How It Works
The Explorer's Method
From Sound to Civilization
Five steps. One method. Infinite discovery.
1

Listen Without Expectation

Each session begins with an unidentified field recording. No labels, no context — just the acoustic artifact itself. Your only tool is your ear.

2

Identify the Region

Using acoustic clues — scale intervals, rhythmic patterns, vocal technique, instrumentation — narrow down the recording's geographic and cultural origin from four possibilities.

3

Receive the Analysis

After your answer, ANTHROPHONY reveals the full acoustic analysis: the exact tradition, its traits, its history, and a Sound DNA map showing its cultural relatives.

4

Unlock the Archive Entry

Each correct identification permanently adds the tradition to your personal museum. Wrong answers still unlock the entry — knowledge is never penalized, only rewarded differently.

5

Follow the Expedition

Traditions link to story-based expeditions — multi-chapter historical journeys that place each sound in the full context of civilizations, migrations, and human memory.

The Archivist

Your AI cultural companion is available at any time. Ask anything about any tradition, region, instrument, or historical period. The Archivist has 4,000+ years of cultural knowledge indexed and ready.

Cookie Policy
Last updated: June 2025

Our Approach to Cookies

ANTHROPHONY uses minimal, privacy-respecting cookies. We believe your cultural exploration data belongs to you, not advertisers.

Essential Cookies

These cookies are required for the platform to function:

  • Session state — remembers your login status during a visit
  • Progress cache — stores your current expedition and game state locally
  • Preference memory — remembers your display and audio settings

Analytics Cookies

We use privacy-first analytics (no third-party tracking, no cross-site identification) to understand how the archive is used. This helps us prioritize which cultural regions to expand. You can opt out at any time through your Profile settings.

No Advertising Cookies

ANTHROPHONY never uses advertising cookies, cross-site tracking pixels, or third-party data brokers. Your exploration of human culture is not a product to be sold.

Managing Cookies

You can clear all ANTHROPHONY cookies at any time through your browser settings. This will reset your local progress cache — your cloud-saved profile will remain intact if you are signed in.
